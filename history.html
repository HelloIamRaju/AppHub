HISTORY OF MATHEMATICS

Ancient Mathematics

Early civilizations such as the Babylonians and Egyptians made foundational contributions to mathematics. The Babylonians developed a base-60 numerical system, and the Egyptians used mathematics for practical tasks like surveying and construction.

Classical Mathematics

The ancient Greeks, including Pythagoras and Euclid, laid the groundwork for geometry and number theory. Pythagoras' theorem and Euclid's axiomatic approach are classic examples from this period.

Medieval Mathematics

During the medieval period, mathematics flourished in the Islamic world. Scholars like Al-Khwarizmi pioneered algebra and introduced the concept of algorithms. Algebraic methods were applied to solve problems in various fields.

Renaissance Mathematics

The Renaissance marked a revival of interest in ancient Greek mathematics. Figures like Leonardo da Vinci and Johannes Kepler contributed to theoretical and applied mathematics. Kepler's laws of planetary motion were a breakthrough in astronomy.

17th Century Mathematics

The 17th century brought the Scientific Revolution, with mathematicians like Newton and Leibniz developing calculus. Newton's laws of motion and universal gravitation revolutionized physics and our understanding of motion and forces.

18th and 19th Century Mathematics

The 18th and 19th centuries saw advancements in abstract algebra, number theory, and non-Euclidean geometry. Gauss contributed to number theory, and Cantor explored the concept of infinity.

20th Century Mathematics

The 20th century brought abstract algebra, set theory, and formal logic to the forefront. Noether's theorem linked symmetries and conservation laws, while Turing's work laid the foundation for computer science.

Modern Mathematics

Modern mathematics includes fields like cryptography, chaos theory, and data analysis. Cryptography plays a vital role in data protection and cybersecurity.
